Badminton

  • The History of Badminton
  • Why Do So Many People Love Playing Badminton?

A lightweight racket and a shuttlecock are used to play the game of badminton, which is often played indoors. In the past, 16 geese were linked to a little cork with a globe and 16 feathers that weighed roughly 5 grams in place of the shuttlecock. The BWF, or Badminton World Federation, permits the use of synthetic materials in the manufacture of shuttles nowadays.

The game of badminton can be played either in singles or doubles, which means that either a single player on each side or double players on each side can compete on the court, which is 13.4 meters long and 6.1 meters wide. In the center of the court is a net. If the shuttlecock strikes the ground after the player hits it, the opponent scores.

The History of Badminton

The country where the dukes of Beaufort lived in England is where badminton gets its name. The first time the game was played in a home was in the year 1873. The origins of the game, which is quite similar to the kids' games shuttlecock and battledore, can be traced to nations like Greece, India, and China.

The badminton global federation, sometimes known as BFW, is the organization in charge of overseeing badminton internationally. The inaugural championship organized by the BWF took place in 1977, and this regulatory organization was founded in the year 1934. While this game is well-known worldwide, it is particularly well-known in Denmark, Japan, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

Why Do So Many People Love Playing Badminton?

Badminton is a well-liked free-time sport since it can be played everywhere, including parks, amusement parks, and gardens. That is also one of the factors that contribute to the popularity of badminton. Which is unsurprising in no way!

Unlike sports that are only played in the winter or summer. The weather has little impact on badminton because it may be played both inside and outside. Players of badminton will not be affected by the weather throughout the year. Then stop whining and begin your subsequent round!

Although badminton is not a physically taxing sport, the technique is more crucial than strength. This implies that everyone, from young toddlers to senior citizens, can play badminton. What wonderful variety!
